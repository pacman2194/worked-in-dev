About me

My name is Drake and I am the owner and proprietor of this establishment. Kinda... What do we really own anymore? I suppose I can claim to own this as long as I keep paying the bills to Microsoft. Either way I'm very excited to have created this very small site to share some thoughts and experiences.

I learned about the wonderful world of "DevOps" in 2018. I didn't really understand what the heck cloud computing even was until then. There seems to be a lack of preparation or information in college on the subject of career paths. Maybe that was just my college. Luckily I really enjoy learning in my free time. When I finally started to learn about it, I realized cloud computing is an amazing democratization of computing that enables large and small companies and individuals all to have access to the same resources. I've become fascinated with the power available to create and manage these resources on a whim. This is not only great for experimentation but also for starting up new companies. Process improvement is a new passion I've also developed since starting my career. I'll definitely be talking a little about that at some point.

I also enjoy my time not working believe it or not. I've got a wonderful girlfriend and we have two dogs. We like to watch obscene amounts of television. Luckily I have good vision insurance. I also like to read comics and books. Yes... I like those picture books. If you haven't read any you should check out 'Bone'. It's an amazing epic story that I think anyone could enjoy. I obviously also like to take some time self-improving by practicing skills and reading articles related to my projects or potential new projects. My friends have also shown me the unfortunately wonderful world of GameBoy emulators and playing homebrewed games. I've also spent a ridiculous amount of time "distro hopping" Linux distros. I'm happy to say I've calmed down and that I run a couple different distros but I don't plan on changing them any time soon. If you're curious wehich ones I've chosen: Manjaro and Debian. I love Arch but Manjaro was just a bit easier to get my desktop going when dual-booting with Windows. Yes, I have Windows because inevitably I like playing games on it that I can't on Linux.

There's plenty of issues in a day I'm sure we could all talk about and this site is in part to take some time to talk about them.
